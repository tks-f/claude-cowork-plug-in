# Writing standards, content guidance & final review

## Writing standards
- Write concise, trustworthy Japanese for a BtoB reader.
- Don't stop at abstractions — make課題, 原因, 対応策, and導入の考え方 concrete.
- Organize the論点 clearly so the reader can reuse it for社内説明.
- Don't over-hype; avoid exaggerated expressions.
- Only propose measures and approaches that are realistically feasible in practice. Avoid
  low-feasibility tactics, thin理想論, or proposals that are hard to operate on the ground.
- If touching on competitor comparison, stay neutral and make it genuine decision material.

## Article goals (every article)
The purpose is to **increase inquiries**. Emphasize:
- Consistent SEO: align search intent, primary keyword, related terms, and heading design.
- Clearly articulate the課題 that決裁者・担当者 face.
- Naturally connect how 株式会社TryWith's services solve that課題.
- Include comparison-useful information, not just selling.
- Lead, ultimately, toward問い合わせ・相談.
- Make the CTA concrete: 資料請求 / 相談 / 問い合わせ — a clear next action.

## Content structure guidance
- Opening: state the reader's課題 and the value the article delivers.
- Middle: organize the background of the課題, how to choose a solution, and practical caveats.
- Introduce 弊社の支援内容 naturally, in context — not as a hard sell.
- End: a call to action toward相談・問い合わせ.
- Where an explanation gets complex, always consider whether a figure would help (see the
  visual-explanation guidance in `deliverable-and-visuals.md`).

Flex these lenses by theme: 開発体制の不足 / ベンダー選定の難しさ / AI導入の現実的な進め方 /
小売・店舗ビジネス特有の業務課題 / 外部CTO・開発支援の活用場面.

## When information is missing
If the user hasn't fully specified keywords, target reader, or the service to feature, infer the
most natural premises from the request and write the draft anyway. Only when a premise strongly
affects quality, show a short「想定」at the top of the draft. Don't stop at questions — return a
usable article draft first.

## Final Japanese review (do this right before final output)
Check and fix:
- Unnatural直訳調 or AI-sounding phrasing.
- Tone that is too stiff or too mechanical.
- Natural Japanese flow, correct subject–predicate agreement, smooth wording.
- Whether it reads as a natural, trustworthy corporate-site article.

The target is writing that reads as a human writer's natural article — not something that feels
AI-generated.
